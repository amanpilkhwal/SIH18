Wildlife

Himachal Pradesh

  • Himachal Pradesh is home to numerous species of plants and animals. With an exhilarating variety of plants, animals and marine life.

  • Himachal wildlife is a look at the wild, raw and ferocious animals that roam freely amongst the rough terrain of Himachal’s mountains, forests and valleys. It has over 33 wildlife sanctuaries and 2 national parks which are biodiverse hotspots for animal lovers, wildlife photographers and researchers.

  • There are also wildlife safaris and camping experiences available. It’s an experience that one should definitely be a part of, because the biodiversity will leave you astounded by the beauty, grace and ferocity of some of the most endangered and rare species.








Wildlife Solution

Things we can do

  • Biodiversity plans need to be prepared and hence development of rescue and veterinary care center for wild fauna should be taken care of.

  • Multi-utility vehicles along with ambulance and communication equipment need to be purchased. These multi-utility vehicles, wireless and GPS sets would be used for patrolling the entire area for protection of animals with reference to the proposal from Chief Wild Life Warden of that area, on integrated development of wildlife habitats.

  • Transportation of water, feed and fodder to the wildlife of the area must be focused upon for their proper health conditions.

  • Trained wildlife experts with adequate rescue and rehabilitation centers and the goodwill of people towards wildlife would certainly improve the situation in the ground level and protect the wildlife which has strayed out of their habitats.

Endangered Animals

Nilgiri Tahr

The Nilgiri tahr is a threatened mountain ungulate clinging on to the tropical rainforest of the Western Ghats. The endangered species have a stocky coat with a bristly mane, curved horns, and coarse and short fur. As per the current survey, there is a total population of 3,000 Nilgiti Tahr in India, inclusive of smaller species which haven’t been documented in the past. The Nilgiri Tahr population is continuously decreasing.

Bengal Tigers

The natural habitat of tigers in India – tropical evergreen forests, deciduous forests, mangrove swamps, thorn forests and grass jungles – has almost disappeared outside the reserves. Altogether, there are 48 tiger reserves in India. But one place that has captured the fancy of major wildlife aficionados is the Jim Corbett National Park. There are currently 2,226 tigers left in India.


Black Buck

Blackbucks, the Indian antelopes, are found in abundance in the plains, grasslands and scrubs of India. Activities like hunting, poaching, habitat destruction, overgrazing and inbreeding has led to a drastic downward fluctuation in the blackbuck population. Another reason why the blackbuck population is immensely decreasing is the large-scale conversion of their habitats into agriculture fields and poaching.

Snow Leopard

Profoundly found in the steep and rocky region of the Himalayas, the snow leopard is the descendant of the wild cat and panther families. There are 6,000 snow leopards in the world, but its population is gradually declining. With the loss of habitat and widespread hunting, there has been a 20% reduction in snow leopard population over the past 20 years. In addition, snow leopards are illegally hunted in the fur trade for making luxury coats and their bones are sought after for traditional Asian medicine.
